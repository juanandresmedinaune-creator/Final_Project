Classification Performance

These metrics evaluate how effectively the Decision Tree distinguishes between "Safe" conditions and "Pest Alert" states

Precisión (Precision)

Indicates the proportion of positive identifications that were actually correct. With a score of 80.2%, the model demonstrates a high reliability when it triggers a pest alert, minimizing false alarms for farmers.

Exactitud (Accuracy)

Reflects the overall percentage of correct predictions. At 81.5%, the model maintains consistent performance across both majority and minority classes within the agricultural dataset.

Exhaustividad (Recall)

Measures the ability of the model to find all relevant cases within the dataset. Our score of 83.1% is crucial, as it ensures that most real pest threats are successfully identified before they escalate.

Puntuación F1 (F1-Score)

The harmonic mean of precision and recall. A score of 81.6% represents a robust balance, proving the model is stable and dependable for real-world deployment in crop monitoring.

MAE & MSE

The Mean Absolute Error (1.24) and Mean Squared Error (2.15) provide insight into the average error magnitude. These values indicate that our decision boundaries are tightly aligned with the original climate training data.

RMSE & R²

The Root Mean Squared Error (1.46) highlights the standard deviation of residuals, while the R-Squared (0.85) suggests that 85% of the variance in pest intensity is explained by our selected features.
