Business Objectives

Six goals that translate real agricultural needs into measurable ML outcomes

Objective 01

Reduce Economic Losses

anticipate pest risk based on current environmental conditions so farmers and agronomists can activate preventive measures before the pest spreads, lowering intervention costs.

Objective 02

Support Agricultural Decision-Making

Provide ICA, Agronet, and coffee grower associations with an interpretable model that classifies infestation risk by municipality and crop type.

Objective 03

Optimize Agrochemical Use

Target phytosanitary applications only to high-risk zones and time windows based on model predictions, reducing costs and environmental impact.

Objective 04

Build a Reproducible Data Pipeline

Integrate open climate data from IDEAM and Ministry of Agriculture records into an automated pipeline that continuously feeds and retrains the model.

Objective 05

Deploy Scalable Early-Warning Alerts

Build a web interface where field technicians enter local variables and get real-time risk predictions by crop type (coffee, rice, banana, etc.).

Objective 06

Validate on Colombian Regional Data

Achieve F1-score ≥ 0.80 on priority crops from Huila, Tolima, Cundinamarca, and the Coffee Region, ensuring the model generalizes across unseen locations.

Key Questions for the Model

Specific questions that guide what the model must predict or explain

🔮

Prediction & Classification

  • What is the probability of a specific pest (e.g., Hypothenemus hampei, coffee rust, whitefly) appearing in a municipality within the next 14 days, based on temperature, humidity, and rainfall?
  • What risk level (low / medium / high / critical) does a plot present based on current climate conditions and historical infestation records?
🌡️

Variables & Feature Importance

  • Which climate variables (max/min temperature, precipitation, wind, soil moisture) are the strongest predictors for each pest type and crop?
  • Is there a climate threshold (e.g., humidity > 80% + temperature 20–25°C for 5 consecutive days) that triggers outbreaks with high statistical confidence?
🗺️

Regional risk estimation by municipality and crop type

  • Which neighboring municipalities are most likely to experience spread once an outbreak is detected, considering wind patterns and farm connectivity?
  • Within what time window does a pest propagate from its focus to exceed the economic damage threshold in adjacent crops?
🗄️

Data Availability & Quality

  • Are ICA and Agronet historical records sufficient in temporal and geographic coverage to train a supervised model with reliable presence/absence labels?
  • How should missing IDEAM climate data in rural low-coverage zones be imputed or handled?
📊

Impact & Evaluation

  • By how much would estimated crop losses decrease if farmers acted on model alerts compared to no predictive intervention (baseline)?
  • How well does the model generalize to unseen crops and pest species driven by climate change?
